The ICC Champions Trophy was a cricket tournament held between the top cricketing nations, organized by the International Cricket Council (ICC). It was contested by the best teams in One Day International (ODI) format. The competition started in 1998 and was typically played every 4 years, often referred to as the "mini World Cup. 
" India, Pakistan, and other top cricketing nations participated in the event. The tournament was discontinued after the 2017 edition, with the ICC focusing on other formats like the World Test Championship and the ICC World Cup.
